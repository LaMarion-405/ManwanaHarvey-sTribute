Manwana (Crazy Wana) Harvey

1958-2024

“ You’ll only lose if you have a losing mindset”

Biography

Manawana Vanessa Harvey was born in the vibrant city of Chicago, a place that would shape her strong and compassionate character. Growing up on the South Side, she became known for her grace, leadership, and deep sense of community. Vanessa was a proud graduate of her high school, where she excelled both academically and as a beloved majorette. Her time as a majorette instilled in her a passion for rhythm, discipline, and teamwork—qualities that would define much of her later life. Vanessa was the proud mother of six children and the loving sister to three siblings. Family was at the heart of everything she did, and her legacy lives on through her 21 grandchildren, all of whom she cherished deeply. She took great pride in watching each of them grow, and their achievements were a source of immense joy and fulfillment for her. Whether it was celebrating graduations, milestones, or simply spending time together, Vanessa's love for her family was unwavering. A particularly special memory for her was the time she spent taking her grandchildren to White Water Bay, where she created lasting memories and joy with them. It was these moments of connection that defined her legacy: a woman who loved deeply and created an environment where her family could thrive. Vanessa's faith in Jesus Christ was the cornerstone of her life. She firmly believed in the guidance of her Savior, trusting that He would lead her through every season of her life, ultimately guiding her home. Her belief in chivalry and respect for others was another key part of who she was. She lived by a set of values that emphasized kindness, compassion, and honor toward others—values that she imparted to her children and grandchildren. In addition to her faith and family, Vanessa was a strong advocate for community service. She devoted much of her time to helping those in need, participating in outreach programs, and assisting the elderly. Whether through offering a helping hand or simply providing a listening ear, she touched the lives of countless people in her community. Her peers admired her not only for her giving spirit but also for her tireless dedication to making the world a better place. Vanessa's life was one of love, service, and unwavering faith. She leaves behind a legacy of kindness, generosity, and family. Her impact will be felt for generations, as her children, grandchildren, and all who knew her continue to carry forward the lessons she taught them: to love deeply, serve selflessly, and trust in God's guidance.
